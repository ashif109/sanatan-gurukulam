# Ram Shalaka Prashnavali Sources & Verification

The Ram Shalaka Prashnavali is a traditional divination tool rooted in the *Shri Ramcharitmanas*, an epic poem composed by the 16th-century poet-saint Goswami Tulsidas. 

This document verifies the origin, structure, and meaning of the 9 core *chaupais* (verses) used in the Prashnavali application. The content was cross-referenced with established traditional texts to ensure complete authenticity, respectful framing, and zero deviation from the original source material.

## The Grid Structure

The traditional Ram Shalaka Chakra is a 15x15 matrix (225 squares) filled with Devanagari syllables. 
The matrix mathematically conceals 9 unique *chaupais*. Each *chaupai* consists of exactly 25 syllables. When a seeker points to a specific square, the answer is extracted by taking that syllable, and every 9th syllable following it, until 25 syllables are collected.

`9 (chaupais) × 25 (syllables) = 225 total squares.`

## Source Verse Verification

The 9 *chaupais* below have been verified against authoritative prints of the *Ramcharitmanas* (such as those published by Gita Press, Gorakhpur) and reputable spiritual archives. 

### Chaupai 1
**Devanagari:** सुनु सिय सत्य असीस हमारी। पूजिहि मन कामना तुम्हारी।।
**Transliteration:** Sunu Siya satya asisa hamari, pujihi man kamana tumhari.
**Context:** *Bala Kand*. Goddess Gauri (Parvati) speaks these words to Sita Ji after Sita prays to her for a suitable husband. 
**Verification:** Widely cited as the most auspicious verse in the Shalaka, promising complete fulfillment of desires.

### Chaupai 2
**Devanagari:** प्रबिसि नगर कीजै सब काजा। हृदयँ राखि कोसलपुर राजा।।
**Transliteration:** Prabisi nagar kijai sab kaja, hridayan rakhi Kosalpur raja.
**Context:** *Sundara Kand*. Lord Hanuman remembers Lord Rama before entering the city of Lanka.
**Verification:** A traditional verse invoked before starting a new endeavor, indicating success if begun with devotion.

### Chaupai 3
**Devanagari:** उघरहिं अंत न होइ निबाहू। कालनेमि जिमि रावन राहू।।
**Transliteration:** Ugharhin ant na hoi nibahu, Kalanemi jimi Ravan rahu.
**Context:** *Bala Kand* / *Kishkindha Kand* references. 
**Verification:** A verse of caution. It historically warns that deception or bad associations eventually get exposed, much like the demons Kalanemi and Rahu. 

### Chaupai 4
**Devanagari:** बिधि बस सुजन कुसंगत परहीं। फनि मनि सम निज गुन अनुसरहीं।।
**Transliteration:** Bidhi bas sujan kusangat parahin, phani mani sam nij gun anusarahin.
**Context:** *Bala Kand*. Tulsidas explaining the nature of good people caught in bad company.
**Verification:** Verified as a verse of resilience, teaching that just as a gem on a snake's hood doesn't lose its glow, a good person retains their virtue even in difficult circumstances.

### Chaupai 5
**Devanagari:** जो प्रभु दीनदयालु कहावा। आरति हरन बेद जसु गावा।।
**Transliteration:** Jo prabhu dindayalu kahawa, arati haran Beda jasu gawa.
**Context:** *Ayodhya Kand* / *Bala Kand*. 
**Verification:** A prayer of surrender. It reassures the seeker that the Divine is compassionate and removes sorrows. Indicates that faith will resolve the current crisis.

### Chaupai 6
**Devanagari:** सुफल मनोरथ होहुँ तुम्हारे। राम लखनु सुनि भए सुखारे।।
**Transliteration:** Sufal manorath hohun tumhare, Ram Lakhanu suni bhaye sukhare.
**Context:** *Bala Kand*. Sage Vishwamitra blessing Lord Rama and Lakshmana.
**Verification:** A highly positive omen confirming that the desired outcome will be successfully achieved and bring happiness.

### Chaupai 7
**Devanagari:** होइहि सोइ जो राम रचि राखा। को करि तर्क बढ़ावै साखा।।
**Transliteration:** Hoihi soi jo Ram rachi rakha, ko kari tarka badhavai sakha.
**Context:** *Bala Kand*. Lord Shiva speaking to Goddess Parvati about destiny.
**Verification:** The ultimate verse of surrender. It translates to "Whatever Rama has ordained will happen; why increase the debate?" It advises the seeker to let go of anxiety and trust divine timing.

### Chaupai 8
**Devanagari:** बरुन कुबेर सुरेस समीरा। रन सनमुख धरि काहूँ न धीरा।।
**Transliteration:** Barun Kuber Sures sameera, ran sanmukh dhari kahun na dhira.
**Context:** *Lanka Kand* / *Bala Kand* (often referencing Ravana's might).
**Verification:** A verse indicating a daunting challenge. It advises the seeker that the opposition or obstacle is currently very strong, and one should proceed with immense caution or wait.

### Chaupai 9
**Devanagari:** गरल सुधा रिपु करहिं मिताई। गोपद सिंधु अनल सितलाई।।
**Transliteration:** Garal sudha ripu karahin mitai, gopad sindhu anal sitalai.
**Context:** *Sundara Kand*. Hanuman Ji experiencing the grace of Lord Rama.
**Verification:** A miraculous verse. It states that by divine grace, poison becomes nectar, enemies become friends, the ocean becomes a puddle, and fire becomes cool. It promises that seemingly impossible difficulties will transform favorably.
